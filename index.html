How a case works

1

A case is revealed. Both sides get three private pieces of evidence — nobody sees the other side's evidence.

2

Over three rounds, each side makes one statement: pick an argument style, back it with an evidence card if you want.

3

The other side decides: let it stand, or object. You won't know how strong the evidence really is until the judge rules — that's the whole game.

4

Some evidence is fake. If you play it and get caught, it costs you badly. If nobody objects, it pays off big.

5

After three rounds, whoever built more credibility wins the case. Points, streaks, and rank carry over between games.
